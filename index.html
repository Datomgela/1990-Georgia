Life in the 1990s in Georgia

1. Can you describe what life was like in Georgia in the 1990s?

- As a result of the collapse of the Soviet Union, 2 wars and then a civil war took place, resulting in economic and social problems. Life became difficult, there was corruption

2. How did Georgian politics affect your daily life at that time?

-As we had to work hard and think about social issues for education we did not have the time and opportunity to develop

3. Has there been any progress or change that stood out for you in the 1990s in Georgia?

- What comes to mind is the Baku-Supsa Oil Pipeline and the foundation was laid by Baku-Tbilisi-Ceyhan in 1998

4. What was it like for you to have fun and relax back then?

-Swimming in the river on holidays, playing football and basketball, hiking in the forest and relaxing in the mountains (Bakhmaro Resort).

5. Can you recall any memorable social or public event in Georgia?

- The tragedy of April 9, 1989. The rally was forcefully dispersed by the Soviet army. 21 people killed and up to 450 injured

6. What were the common sectors that people in Georgia were engaged in in the 1990s?

-Agricultural work, trade and driving

7. What was your main food?

-Beans, corn, cheese, chicken

8. Was there any particular challenge or hardship you faced in the 1990s in Georgia?

-There were no jobs and minimum wages and pensions would be issued with a delay

9. What was your daily routine in Georgia in the 1990s?

-I went to school to interact with friends with relatives and was engage in sports

10. What was the reason for the resilience and spirit of the Georgian people during the turbulent 1990s?

-Despite the hardships and trust that helped us overcome the problems.
